# Business & Strategy Master Guide

Integrated framework for professional business engineering, consolidating market intelligence, product-led growth, financial modeling, and venture readiness.

## 1. Market Intelligence & Sizing

### TAM / SAM / SOM Methodology
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: The total market demand for a product or service. Use top-down (industry reports) and bottom-up (Price × # of potential customers) to triangulate.
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: The segment of the TAM within your geographical reach and product capabilities.
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in the short-to-medium term.
- **Validation**: Use the **Value Theory** approach for new categories: "How much value are we creating? How much can we capture?"

### Competitive Analysis
- **Porter’s Five Forces**: Analyze Threat of New Entrants, Bargaining Power of Buyers/Suppliers, Threat of Substitutes, and Competitive Rivalry.
- **Blue Ocean Strategy**: Identify "Value Innovation" by simultaneously pursuing differentiation and low cost to create uncontested market space.
- **SWOT / PESTEL**: Standardize on decision-oriented summaries—don't just list facts, identify the *strategic implication* of each quadrant.

## 2. Product Management & Discovery

### Prioritization Frameworks
- **RICE Scoring**: `(Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort`.
  - *Reach*: Users per quarter.
  - *Impact*: 3 (Massive) to 0.25 (Minimal).
  - *Confidence*: 100% (High) to 50% (Low).
- **MoSCoW**: Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have.
- **North Star Metric**: Identify the single metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to customers.

### PRD & Discovery
- **Hypothesis Template**: "We believe [feature] for [users] will achieve [outcome]. We'll know we're right when [metric]."
- **PRD Standards**: Must include Problem Statement, User Stories, Success Metrics, Technical Constraints, and a Go-to-Market (GTM) plan.
- **Customer Discovery**: Follow the "The Mom Test" principles: ask about specific past behavior, not hypothetical future features.

## 3. Growth, Pricing & Monetization

### Unit Economics & Metrics
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: `ARPU × Gross Margin % / Churn Rate`.
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: `Total S&M Spend / New Customers Acquired`.
- **LTV/CAC Ratio**: Aim for >3x for a healthy venture. Payback period should ideally be <12 months.
- **Churn Analysis**: Distinguish between *Logo Churn* and *Net Revenue Retention (NRR)*. NRR > 100% (negative churn) is the gold standard for SaaS.

### Pricing Strategy
- **Van Westendorp PSM**: Determine price sensitivity through four questions (Too Cheap, Cheap, Expensive, Too Expensive).
- **Value-Based Pricing**: Price based on the customer's perceived value rather than cost-plus or competitor-matching.
- **Tiers & Packaging**: Use **Good/Better/Best** tiers. Ensure the "Middle" tier is the most attractive anchor for most users.

## 4. Investor Readiness & Financials

### Pitch Deck Structure
1. **The Wedge**: Why now? What is the immediate entry point?
2. **Problem/Solution**: Clear pain point vs. unique value proposition.
3. **Product/Demo**: Visual proof of execution.
4. **Market/Competition**: TAM/SAM/SOM + differentiation map.
5. **Business Model**: How you make money + Unit Economics.
6. **Traction**: Growth curves, cohort analysis, and milestones.
7. **The Ask**: Amount, terms (if applicable), and specific use of funds.

### Financial Modeling
- **3-Statement Model**: Integrated Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow.
- **Burn & Runway**: Calculate `Gross Burn` (total spend) vs. `Net Burn` (spend - revenue).
- **Scenario Planning**: Always present "Base", "Upside", and "Downside" cases.

## 5. Marketing Psychology & CRO
- **Anchoring**: Present a high-priced "Enterprise" tier first to make "Pro" tiers seem affordable.
- **Social Proof**: Use logos, testimonials, and "Trust Bars" near conversion points.
- **Loss Aversion**: Highlight what the user *loses* by not switching or upgrading (e.g., "Stop losing $X/mo in inefficient spend").

## 6. Business Strategy Checklist

- [ ] **Market**: Bottom-up TAM/SAM/SOM calculated and triangulated with top-down data.
- [ ] **Competitive**: Direct and indirect competitors mapped by differentiation levers, not just feature lists.
- [ ] **Product**: North Star Metric defined and aligned with the "Aha!" moment.
- [ ] **Metrics**: LTV/CAC and Payback Period verified with cohort-level data.
- [ ] **Pricing**: Value metric (e.g., per user, per GB) aligned with customer success.
- [ ] **Financials**: Monthly burn rate and runway confirmed; hiring plan tied to revenue/milestone triggers.
- [ ] **Investor**: 12-slide pitch deck source-of-truth established and consistent with the financial model.
- [ ] **CRO**: Pricing page implements anchoring and clear "Most Popular" call-to-action.
